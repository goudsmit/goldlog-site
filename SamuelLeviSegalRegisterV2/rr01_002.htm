Family of Samuel b Levi Segal (forefather of) Goudsmit Bloemhof Groenewoudt (1) & Eva b Mozes (Mozes)

2. Jonas b Samuel Goudsmit (Levi Segal Goldschmidt). Born on 4.5.1739 in Germany, Frankfurt Region. died on 18.2.1817 in Oud-Beijerland. Buried in Oud-Beijerland. Occupation: Koopman, Winkelier, Bankhouder.

22.3.1771 Oisterwijk: earliest record found sofar of Jonas Samuel, in a municipal certificate of good behaviour (Oisterwijk Oud Rechterlijk Archief inv nr 511): He had been living during 2 years in the house of David Hartog
1780 Eersel -- his signature in Hebrew: Jona bar Sjemoel Segal
1811 Lommel -- he is registered (for the first time) with surname Goudsmit
1812 moving with his whole family from Lommel, via Oisterwijk to Oud-Beijerland, house nr 358 Molendijk
[NB: notifier in his death act is his foster son Benjamin Levi Hijmans (33) stated as his “neeff” (= cousin!), which might mean that Benjamin’s father Hijman Levi was another brother of Jonas. But there is no other proof for this and the word cousin is sometimes used for kinship in a general meaning]

abt 1768 Jonas b Samuel first married Hanna/Gelle b Samson Levi in Oisterwijk? Born abt 1740 in Oisterwijk? died abt 1793 in Eersel.

in some records mentionned as Johanna Simon Levi or Kaatje Samuels
[NB, uncertain assumption: As the hebrew (signature) name of her second son Simon is “Simcha” (a seldom seen version for Simon) she might well be the sister of the gabbay Marcus Simon (Mordechai bar Simcha) in Oisterwijk, where she may have met her husband; another fact sustaining this assumption is that the first time that Jonas is registered in the taxlists of Veghel (1771), he is wrongly stated as “Jonas Moses Simons”, in later years as “Jonas Samuel, Jew”]

They had the following children:
7 i. Samuel b Jonas Goudsmit (Levi / Segal) (20.11.1769-16.4.1845)
8 ii. Simon b Jonas Goudsmit (Levi / Segal) (1773-12.2.1834)

10 iv. NN2 b Jonas (Goudsmit) Levi / Segal (Died as Child) (abt 1777-bef 1790)
11 v. Jacob b Jonas Goudsmit (Levi / Segal) (11.4.1779-7.8.1869)

Born on 10.9.1756 in s-Heerenberg, Bergh. died on 6.3.1843 in Oud-Beijerland. Buried in Oud-Beijerland. Occupation: Koopvrouw (1820, 1827).

also stated as Schona

4. Meijer/Abraham b Samuel (Levi) Bloemhof. Born in 1745 in Germany, Wetterau Region (north of Frankfurt). died on 26.2.1828 in Eersel. Buried in Eersel.

since 1811 (NaamsAanneming) recorded under his new first name Abraham and new surname Bloemhof. Though later his old first name Meijer was stated again in several instances and Bloemhof was spelled in many variations (Blomhoff etc). [It is rather unusual to choose a surname differing from his oldest brother’s, Jonas Samuel Goudsmit, but by taking the new first name Abraham it seems like he considered his NameAssumption as the new beginning of his Family-branch; and also he may have found it difficult to associate his profession as a Butcher with the name Goudsmit, which means Goldsmith.]

5. Jacob b Samuel Levi Groenewoudt. Born in 1749 in Germany, Reiskirchen (in Buseck Tal, near Giessen). died on 14.10.1827 in Steenbergen. Buried in Oudenbosch. Occupation: Slagter (1818).

[Rather unusual to choose a surname differing from his 2 older brothers: Goudsmit & Bloemhof. Anyway, Jacob Samuel didn’t assume a (new) surname in 1811. In acts from 1814 and 1815 he’s still named Jacob Levy. Only in his death act he is stated with the double surname Levy Groenewoud -- maybe because meanwhile his son Samuel had elsewhere registered the new name Groenewoudt. When and where is so far not found. His daughter Eva is found for the first time stated with the name Groenewoud in a notarial act dd 1820 in Tholen]

abt 1778 Jacob b Samuel first married Lena b Andries Lion, daughter of Andries b Joseph Lion (abt 1730-) & Catharina b David Gans (abt 1730-), in Hilvarenbeek? Born in 1748 in Hilvarenbeek. died on 22.4.1811 in Steenbergen.
