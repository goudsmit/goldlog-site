Third Generation
Family of Levinus/Levi b Jacob Sluis (Sluijs) (2) & Roosje b NN NN

12. Belia b Levinus Sluis (Sluijs). Born abt 1688 in Huissen?

[NB: (in margin of her marriage act) “geboren tot Husse in ‘t land van Cleef”. This might well be Huissen, a town that belonged to the Duchy of Kleve until 1803, and is nowadays a part of the Netherlands]

On 23.1.1724 Belia b Levinus married Jacob b Salomon van Dorth, son of Salomon b NN van Dorth (abt 1650-bef 1724) & Spouse of Salomon van Dorth (abt 1650-), in Dordrecht. Born abt 1690 in Dordrecht?

[NB: in his marriage act he is stated as ijacques Salomons]
2.1.1724 Amsterdam: “On 6 Teveth 5484 appear before the trustees of the jewish community in Amsterdam the brothers aluf khr"r Judaleib and naaleh Abraham, sons of the late khr"r Shlomo Dort z.l, and take the obligation to supply their sister-in-law Beile bat naaleh kh r"r Judaleib Shlis m.h.l, spouse of their brother Jacob Jokeb with a chalitsa, might it be needed.” 5

They had the following children:
26 i. Isaac b Jacob van Dorth (abt 1730-)
27 ii. Abigael b Jacob van Dorth (1738-)
28 iii. Roosje b Jacob Sluijs van Dorth (1739-3.6.1820)

13. Marcus b Levinus Sluis (Sluijs). Born abt 1695 in Maassluis?

25.12.1728 Dordrecht: He is one of the initiators and signatories of a notarial act founding and regulating the jewish community of Dordrecht (together with his father Levinus Jacob Sluijs, his brother Salomon Sluijs, his uncle Hartog Jacob and his cousin Zagharias Eliazar) 4
1737 Dordrecht: Marcus Sluijs takes the initiative for the jewish cemetary
1739 Dordrecht: Marcus Sluijs takes the initiative for the synagogue

On 25.5.1725 Marcus b Levinus married Aaltje b Abraham Cohen in Rotterdam. Born abt 1695 in Rotterdam?

also stated as Aaltje Andries Cohen (Edil b Anshil Cohen)

They had the following children:
29 i. Sara b Marcus Sluis (Sluijs) (1728-4.4.1827)
30 ii. Ansel b Marcus Sluis (Sluijs) (abt 1730-)
31 iii. Vrouwtje b Marcus Sluis (Sluijs) (1738-)

14. Abraham b Levinus Sluis (Sluijs). Born abt 1695 in Dordrecht?

In 1721 Abraham b Levinus married Abigael b Salomon in Amsterdam. Born in 1705 in Amsterdam.

daughter of Salomon Jacob

They had one child:
32 i. Salomon b Abraham Sluis (Sluijs) (1723-)

15. Salomon b Levinus Sluis (Sluijs). Born abt 1700 in Dordrecht?

26 april 1726: Levinis Jacobs Sluijs, "sijnde van de Joodsche natie", wonende te Dordrecht, verklaart toe te stemmen in het huwelijk van zijn zoon Salomon Sluijs met  Ariaentje Nathans Wolff, jonge dochter wonende te Rotterdam, "op de Joodsche wijse en volgens de ordre deser Landen gebruijckelijck". (OudNotarieelArchiefDordrecht 619, f. 74) 3
25.12.1728 Dordrecht: He is one of the initiators and signatories of a notarial act founding and regulating the jewish community of Dordrecht (together with his father Levinus Jacob Sluijs, his brother Marcus Sluijs, his uncle Hartog Jacob and his cousin Zagharias Eliazar) 4
11 aug. 1730: comp. voor notaris B. van der Star: Salomon Sluijs, Joods koopman te Dordrecht. Hij is wegens geleende penningen een bedrag van 340 gl. schuldig aan Maarten Veen, burger van Dordrecht. Borg: zijn vader Livinus Jacobsz. Sluijs, Joods koopman te Dordrecht. Akte door beiden met Latijnse letters ondertekend. (OudNotarieelArchiefDordrecht 856, akte 59) 3

On 3.6.1726 Salomon b Levinus married Adriana b Nathan Wolf Cohen in Rotterdam. Born abt 1700 in Rotterdam.

They had the following children:
33 i. Marcus b Salomon Sluis (Sluijs) (1.1734-17.11.1813)
34 ii. Clara b Salomon Sluis (Sluijs) (abt 1735-)
35 iii. Judic b Salomon Sluis (Sluijs) (1751-15.8.1833)
